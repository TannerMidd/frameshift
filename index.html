Your Elite Dangerous cockpit, on every screen.

Frameshift reads your journal in real time and serves a live cockpit — trading, exploration, engineering and mission planning — from your PC to any paired tablet or browser. Local-first: no account, no API key, no cloud.

Mission capabilities

LIVE COCKPIT

Real-time flight status from your journal: fuel, cargo, rebuy cover, missions, exploration value at risk — with focused voice callouts and one-tap autoplot that types the route into Elite with your own keybinds and verifies it against NavRoute.json.

TRADING & MARKETS

A local galaxy market database (Spansh seed + live EDDN) answers loop and multi-hop searches in seconds, ranked by profit per hour with an honest confidence score and conservative range on every result. Commodity search, mining buyers, price history, persistent watches.

EXPLORATION & EXOBIOLOGY

Road to Riches, neutron routing, bio prediction with community-known signals, first-log ×5 estimates, a surface sample navigator with clearance distances, sell-data finder and per-body waypoint maps that survive restarts.

ENGINEERING

A complete offline catalog — 1,100+ recipes: ship blueprints, experimentals, engineer and tech-broker unlocks, Odyssey upgrades. Wishlists with material deficits, trader math and pinned-plan steps that feed straight into your session plan.

GALAXY & MISSION CONTROL

Powerplay 2.0 pledge and merits, BGS factions, conflicts and reputation on every arrival — plus a time-budgeted session planner that turns missions, deadlines and pinned work into a plot-ready plan using durations learned from your own flying.

SPECIALIST WORKSPACES

Dedicated flows for mining sessions, combat & AX readiness, fleet-carrier upkeep and tritium routes, colonisation hauling with parallel source-finding, and shareable squadron ops boards — exchanged as plain JSON files, no service involved.

Even startup is part of the cockpit — the initiation sequence replays your latest flight logs

Make it react your way — no code

The experimental extension builder turns “I wish it told me when…” into a working alert in about a minute.

  • Pick a game event, add conditions like “Reward is at least 100,000”
  • Cockpit alert with optional voice callout, or a suggested objective
  • Test against your own flight history before saving — see exactly what would have fired
  • Saved as a plain local pack: reviewable, editable, removable — and it can never execute code

Three steps to a glass cockpit
STEP 01

Download & run

One Windows exe (or run from source on Linux/Steam Deck). It finds your journal folder and commander automatically and keeps itself updated.

STEP 02

Build the market database

One click in Settings downloads the galaxy snapshot; live EDDN keeps it fresh from then on. Trading, mining and station search unlock galaxy-wide.

STEP 03

Pair your tablet

Scan a one-time QR code from Settings. The tablet gets the touch-first Panel view with its own pages, themes and permissions — revocable any time.

Game not running? One guarded tap handles ignition — launch control stands down once you're flying

LOCAL-FIRST

No account, no API key, no cloud dependency. Commander history, plans and settings live on your disk, separated from the rebuildable market cache, with automatic backups.

FAIR TO THE NETWORK

Anonymous market observations go back to EDDN by default; broader exploration reporting is a separate opt-in, validated against the official schemas.

PLAYS WELL WITH OTHERS

Runs happily alongside EDMC, Inara, EDSM and friends — links out to them rather than pretending they don't exist.

EXTENSIBLE, SAFELY

Local extension packs can react to journal events declaratively — no code execution unless you approve an adapter's exact fingerprint.

Standing on community shoulders

Frameshift exists because the Elite Dangerous community shares its data: EDDN live observations, Spansh galaxy data and routing, Inara and EDSM reference links, and EDCD schemas and documentation. Bundled open-source work: EDEngineer reference data, Project Nayuki's QR generator and Piper TTS. If these projects help you, support them.
